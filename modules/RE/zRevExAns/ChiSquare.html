Distribution of Cyprinids in Asia

  1. α = 0.05.
  2. The H0: “The proportion of fish from the Cyprinidae family is the same for all three rivers” versus HA: “The proportion of fish from the Cyprinidae family is NOT the same for all three rivers.”
  3. A chi-square test is required because (i) a categorical variable with two levels (cyprinidae and other) was measured on (ii) three groups/populations (rivers).
  4. The data appear to be part of an observational study. There is no indication that the fish were randomly selected (they likely were not, though hopefully the locations for collecting the fish were).
  5. The expected frequencies are in (Table 1). The test statistic below should follow a χ2 distribution because the expected number in each cell is greater than five.
  6. The appropriate statistic is the observed frequency table in (Table 2).
  7. The χ2 test statistic is \(\frac{(22-24.65351)^{2}}{24.65351}\) + \(\frac{(51-48.34649)^{2}}{48.34649}\) + \(\frac{(25-26.34211)^{2}}{26.34211}\) + \(\frac{(53-51.65789)^{2}}{51.65789}\) + \(\frac{(30-26.00439)^{2}}{26.00439}\) + \(\frac{(47-50.99561)^{2}}{50.99561}\) = 0.2856027 + 0.06837899 + 0.6139323 + 0.1456385 + 0.03486876 + 0.3130648 = 1.4615 with (2-1) × (3-1) = 2 df.
  8. The p-value is 0.4816.
  9. The null hypothesis is not rejected because the p-value > α.
  10. There is no apparent difference in the proportion of fish from Cyprinidae family among the three rivers (Table 3).
  11. Not needed for a chi-square test.
