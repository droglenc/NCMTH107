Length of Northern Pike

  1. α = 0.05. (Note that this is obtained from working backwards from the 95% confidence statement.)
  2. H0: μ = 600, HA: μ ≠ 600 where μ is the mean length of all pike in Chivyrkui Bay, Lake Baikal.
  3. A one-sample Z-test is required because a (i) quantitative variable (length) was measured, (ii) individuals from only one group/population were sampled (Chivyrkui Bay, Lake Baikal), and (iii) σ is known (given in the background).
  4. Observational study with n = 30 pike. No obvious randomization.
  5. n = 30 ≥ 15 and the population is not strongly skewed (background says “slightly”" right-skewed); σ is known.
  6. x¯ = 656.1.
  7. Z = \(\frac{656.1-600}{\frac{130}{\sqrt{30}}}\) = 2.363.
  8. The p-value is 0.0181.
  9. Reject H0 because the p-value<α.
  10. The true mean length of all pike in Lake Baikal appears to be greater than 600 mm. (You strictly tested for a difference, but the x¯ clearly shows that the mean length is greater than 600 mm.)
  11. One is 95% confident that the true mean length of all pike in Lake Baikal is between 609.6 and 702.6 mm (from 656.1 ± 1.96*23.74, 656.1 ± 46.53). This further supports that the mean length of Lake Baikal pike is greater than 600 mm.

Chronic Myofascial Pain Syndrome

  1. α = 0.10.
  2. H0: μ = 2.5, HA: μ > 2.5 where μ is the mean length of pain.
  3. A one-sample Z-test is required because (i) a quantitative variable (length of pain) was measured, (ii) individuals from only one group/population were sampled (patients with this syndrome), and (iii) σ is known (given in the background).
  4. An observational study that is not obviously random was used.
  5. The population is normally distributed (given in the background) and σ is known (= 0.5). Thus, the test statistic below should follow a normal distribution.
  6. x¯ = 2.77 years (Table 1).
  7. Z = 1.31 (Table 1).
  8. p-value = 0.0957 (Table 1).
  9. Reject H0 because the p-value<α.
  10. It appears that the mean duration of pain for all patients may be longer than 2.5 years.
  11. One is 90% confident that the mean duration of pain for all patients is more than 2.51 years (Table 1), which further supports (just barely) that the mean is greater than 2.5.

Cholesterol Levels

  1. α = 0.10.
  2. H0: μ = 190, HA: μ ≠ 190 where μ is the mean cholesterol level of Asian women.
  3. A one-sample Z-test is required because (i) a quantitative variable (cholesterol level) was measured, (ii) individuals from only one group/population were sampled (Asian women aged 21-40 that had recently immigrated to the U.S.), and (iii) σ is known (given in the background).
  4. An observational study with randomly selected women was used.
  5. n = 40 ≥ 30 and σ is known.
  6. x¯ = 180.55 mg/dl (Table 2).
  7. Z = -1.49 (Table 2).
  8. p-value = 0.1352 (Table 2).
  9. The H0 is not rejected because the p-value>α.
  10. The true mean cholesterol level for all Asian women aged 21-40 that recently immigrated to the U.S. does not appear to be different than 190 mg/dl.
  11. One is 90% confident that the mean cholesterol level for all Asian women aged 21-40 that recently immigrated to the U.S. is between 170.15 and 190.96 mg/dl (Table 2), which further supports (just barely) that the mean is not different from 190 mg/dl.
