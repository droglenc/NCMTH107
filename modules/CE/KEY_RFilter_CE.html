Note:
  • When you filter a data.frame, you should name it (to the left of <-) something different than the original name. Thay way you can always return to your original data.frame if needed (as needed here).
  • Use str() to observe the structure of the data.frame which will, among other things, show the number of individuals (labeled as obs).

Coarse Woody Debris

  1. There are nine rows of only low exposure sites (see lowexp object below).
  2. There are still nine rows of only low exposure sites (see lowexp2 object below).
  3. There are 13 rows of sites with diameters greater than 20 cm (see gt20 object below).
  4. There are four rows of low exposure sites with diameters greater than 20 cm (see lowexpgt20 object below).
  5. There are nine rows of sites with diameters between 20 and 30 cm (see btw2030 object below).

R Code and Results

> cwdobj <- read.csv("cwd.csv")
> str(cwdobj)
'data.frame':   50 obs. of  2 variables:
 $ diameter: int  21 15 18 23 18 17 19 17 15 22 ...
 $ exposure: chr  "med" "med" "med" "low" ...
> lowexp <- filterD(cwdobj,exposure=="low")
> str(lowexp)
'data.frame':   9 obs. of  2 variables:
 $ diameter: int  23 17 16 24 19 34 16 16 34
 $ exposure: chr  "low" "low" "low" "low" ...
> lowexp2 <- filterD(cwdobj,exposure!="med")
> str(lowexp2)
'data.frame':   9 obs. of  2 variables:
 $ diameter: int  23 17 16 24 19 34 16 16 34
 $ exposure: chr  "low" "low" "low" "low" ...
> gt20 <- filterD(cwdobj,diameter>20)
> str(gt20)
'data.frame':   13 obs. of  2 variables:
 $ diameter: int  21 23 22 24 24 23 31 25 34 23 ...
 $ exposure: chr  "med" "low" "med" "low" ...
> lowexpgt20 <- filterD(cwdobj,exposure=="low",diameter>20)
> str(lowexpgt20)
'data.frame':   4 obs. of  2 variables:
 $ diameter: int  23 24 34 34
 $ exposure: chr  "low" "low" "low" "low"
> btw2030 <- filterD(cwdobj,diameter>20,diameter<30)
> str(btw2030)
'data.frame':   9 obs. of  2 variables:
 $ diameter: int  21 23 22 24 24 23 25 23 24
 $ exposure: chr  "med" "low" "med" "low" ...
